Generated on: 19 March 2026

I use tool every single day without paying for it, without asking for
permission, and without waiting for a company to decide what features I
should or should not have. That is not a small thing. To me, freedom in
software means privacy — the ability to look under the hood, change
what doesn't work, and share what you've learned.

If I had the skill and time, I would build netflix and put it out there
for anyone who needs it. Not because it would make me money, but because
that is how the tools I depend on were made — by people who gave something
without knowing exactly who would benefit from it.

Open source is not charity. It is a choice about what kind of world you
want to work in. I would rather work in one where knowledge is shared.

-- tanishq, 19 March 2026
